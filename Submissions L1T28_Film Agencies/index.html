An Overview of the industry

South Africa has a vibrant and well-established film industry, producing all types of local and international work year-round.

Due to the favourable exchange rate for US, UK and EU clients, SA is an attractive filming destination. But what really makes it so incredibly popular is that, despite saving money on crew, international clients can rely on South Africa providing an industry consisting of incredibly skilled technicians and stellar crew who not only service the local South African industry, but the entire African continent and are sought-after well beyond.

Due to their infrastructure, economic diversity, access to rental gear and recognized skilled work-force, Johannesburg and Cape Town are by far the busiest cities of the film industry, though Durban also has a far smaller yet healthy scene producing mostly local content.

Johannesburg
The City of Gold

Johannesburg is a popular shooting location all year round, offering sunny yet rain-free shooting conditions in winter, and longer nights in which to shoot night-scenes.

Within a 60km radius, a film shoot has access to:

  • nature reserves and game reserves
  • large bodies of water, such as the Vaal Dam
  • gritty inner-city scapes, middle-class suburbia, posh Sandton mansions, established townships steeped in the revolutionary history of this country, industrial hubs on the edges of the city
  • world-class infrastructure, with the exception of the unstable supply of electricity.
    Not familiar? See what load-shedding and Johannesburg's answer to the struggle of supplying the country with power are here.
  • access to the world-class gear houses, such as the titan Panavision, and excellent local gear houses such as Media Film Service and others.
Cape Town
The Mother City

The film industry in Cape Town is seasonal.

In Spring, Summer and for a little while into Autumn, Cape Town is the most popular filming destination for international clients due to its long summer days, allowing exterior day shoots to take advantage of the natural sunlight from 05:00 to 23:00.

During this time, it is not uncommon to drive through the city and find multiple unrelated shoots underway simultaneously, often a couple of street corners from each other.

Cape Town winters, however, are marked by experiencing "four seasons in a day", putting extreme strain on exterior shoots due to the frequent and often volatile changes in weather, often marked by downpours of rain blown sideways by the strong winds. These conditions are of course unfavourable in trying to maintain the safety of cast, crew, equipment and maintaining the established look of a shoot throughout the day.

Cape Town also offers a uniquely easy-to-access variety of geographic locations.

Within a 60km radius, a film shoot has access to:

  • The Mountain (Table Mountain and its Nature Reserve)
  • the mountain passes of Grabouw
  • the vast "desert" dunes of Atlantis
  • the beaches and oceans of the Atlantic and Pacific?????
  • every sort of architecture - from the most opulant modern beach villas, grand old farm mansions and vast vineyards to middle-class suburbs steeped in history and the sprawling townships, to rural subsistace-farming villages and untouched nature
  • a multitude of film studios (see www.filmcapetown.com for a comprehensive list)
  • world-class infrastructure, with the exception of the unstable supply of electricity.

  • access to the world-class gear houses, such as the titan Panavision, and excellent local gear houses such as Media Film Service and others.
